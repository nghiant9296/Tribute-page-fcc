Here's a time line of Frank Lampard's life

  • 1978 - Born in london, England
  • 1997-1998 Lampard began his career at West Ham United, He secured a place in the first team.
  • 2001 - He moved to rival London club Chelsea for £11 million.
  • 2004-2007 - He established himself as a prolific scorer at the west London club and was a key part of the sides which won back-to-back Premier League titles.
  • 2008 - He signed a new contract, becoming the highest-paid Premier League footballer at that time,[13] and scored in his first Champions League Final that year.
  • 2010 - Lampard helped Chelsea secure their first league and FA Cup Double, and also had his most prolific season with the club, scoring 22 league goals and 17 league assists.
  • 2012 - Lampard captained Chelsea to their first UEFA Champions League success.
  • 2013 - Lampard captained the London club to a 2–1 victory over Benfica, securing the 11th major trophy of the Roman Abramovich era.
  • 2014 - Lampard scored 250th goal of his club career in a 3–0 win against Stoke. Lampard was included on the list of players released by Chelsea and Chelsea confirmed Lampard would leave the club.
  • 2015 - Lampard signed a pre-contract to play for New York City FC in MLS, beginning on 1 July.
  • 2016 - New York City FC announced that Lampard would leave as soon as his contract expired.
  • 2017 - Lampard announced his retirement on 2 February 2017 after turning down several offers, and revealed that he would look to obtain his coaching qualifications after retiring.
